About

In her work Sophie Bauhaus is concerned with questions of control and transformation. She is interested in the dynamics between the desire to determine the outcome of a process and the inability to decide upon certain results. Holding on to existing structures opposes change and transformation. Both on the individual and personal level, as well as on the societal level, everybody and everything is subject to change and transformation constantly. On both levels there is an ongoing conflict between forces that try to remain in existing realities, and forces that are willing to take risks in order to advance. Surrendering to this fact and the engagement in this reality form the core of her creative work.

Sophie Bauhaus is looking at experiences made in a society,which is still dominated by this conflict between forces committed to avoid change and forces that seek to improve the situation of the disadvantaged. More specifically, she is looking at the painful confrontation with patriarchal society from a feminist perspective. In her current series she is working on the visualisation of emotions and anxieties, to explore how they look like and what forms and colours they take. And also to find out how a better place could look like.
